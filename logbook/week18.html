Week 18: Small plasmids recovery issue

Overview

  • Flye stuggle to recover small plasmids i think because of two reason:
    • dinamic value minOverlap (using N90) paramter in disjointig step and collapsing step oftenly higher than the small plasmid size
    • high copy number of small plasmids will create high coverage in short sequence and Flye identified those as noise
  • Concatemer contigs observed in the assembly results are originally from the raw reads.

Small plasmids recovery issue

An unexpected issue in the assembly assessment: For the same sample and the same tool combination, 50x and 100x depth assemblies sometimes miss small plasmids that the 20x depth dataset successfully recovers.

I performed the following initial investigation:

  • Breadth of coverage verification: When the high depth datasets missed the small plasmids but the low depth recovered them, the low depth sample still had excellent base coverage (>92%).

  • This “high depth misses, low depth recovers” issue is entirely exclusive to the ATCC_25922__202309 E. coli sample, to be precise is contig plasmid_4 (1919bp). Pairwise alignment confirmed that plasmid_4 does not share high sequence similarity (~35%) with plasmid_3 (3173bp).

So, I focused further investigation exclusively on the E. coli datasets.

  • Algorithm Determinism: To ensure whether this issue because Flye’s randomness, I manually reran Flye 10 times for two processed datasets (seqkit-barbell and seqkit-dorado) and the unprocessed-untrimmed dataset across the three downsampling depths. Each dataset consistently produced the exact same result regarding whether it recovered the small plasmids or not across all 10 iterations.

  • So, I think this small plasmids issue is not due to randomness in the Flye algorithm decision, but is fundamentally tied to how the Flye algorithm processes high copy number and/or short plasmid sequences.

Flye Struggle with small plasmids

My premise : Flye is inherently struggle with small plasmid recovery.

Findings from literature and GitHub issues

There are mutiple evidence or finding that oftenly Flye stuggle to recover small plasmid, especially in the high depth sample:

  • There is ongoing issue about small plasmid recovery on Flye https://github.com/mikolmogorov/Flye/issues/706

  • High depth impact small plasmid recovery (https://www.microbiologyresearch.org/content/journal/mgen/10.1099/mgen.0.001024#F3). As copy numbers increase and depth becomes too high, Flye may treat these elements as “noise” maybe from barcode/adapter? hmmm but i think no adapter/barcode have sequence length more than 1kb?

Investigating how exactly Flye builds the assembly

I read through the Flye paper explaining their method on how to assembly genome from long read data. In the disjointig generation step, when looking to overlap each read and concatenate them together, the authors mention:

Let Alignments = Alignments(Genome, minOverlap) be the set of all sufficiently long (of length at least ‘minOverlap’) self-alignments of a genome ‘Genome’. Flye sets the ‘minOverlap’ parameter as the N90 of the read-set (the N90 of reads is the largest possible number N such that all reads of length N or longer have a total length of at least 90% of the total sequence; ‘minOverlap’ varies from 3,000 to 5,000 nucleotides for the SMS datasets analyzed in this paper).

  • Also, Flye only collapses repeating sequences into a single node if the repeat length is strictly longer than this minOverlap threshold. Most of the high depth datasets that missed the small contigs have a fastq N90 that slightly higher than the plasmid_4 length (1919 bp).

  • Also, Flye strictly uses minOverlap when performing disjointig assembly. It will only concatenate reads if the overlap between them is longer than the dynamic minOverlap threshold.

Why the failure is not universal across tool combinations

If it is true that Flye fundamently struggles with small plasmid recovery due to its minOverlap and high coverage. Why failure is not universal across our specific tool combinations?

If we look at the contig length plot below, the plasmid_4 contigs in the assembly frequently create artificial dimers (2x, 3838 bp) or trimers (3x, 5757 bp) depending on the preprocessing pipeline used
